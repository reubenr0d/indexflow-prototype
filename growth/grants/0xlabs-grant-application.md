| What are your project's/product's 3 key KPIs?                                                | **Chain-local KPIs** (ring-fenced per deployment for ecosystem attribution): 1. TVL per chain deployment (reserve depth + allocated capital). 2. Trading volume on the shared perpetual liquidity layer. 3. Fee generation (management fees on AUM + trading fees). **Protocol-level KPIs** (tracked in aggregate without blending chain-attributed pilot KPIs): 1. Protocol-retained fee revenue (fees accruing to the protocol treasury under the documented fee split; distinct from gross fee generation attributed to a chain partner). 2. What remains unresolved is how to combine these advances into a product that is easy to own, honest about redemption, and measurable as infrastructure. IndexFlow sits at that intersection. Additionally, chain ecosystems are increasingly seeking attributable liquidity programs that produce durable value rather than short-lived incentive-driven TVL spikes.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         |
| What is the value proposition to the blockchain ecosystem?                                   | ndexFlow provides a controlled liquidity pilot programme that lets a chain buy growth with full attribution. Each deployment is ring-fenced per chain with independent KPI tracking (TVL, volume, fees). This is not a treasury subsidy -- it is infrastructure deployment. Deeper reserves drive higher redemption confidence, which drives more deposits, which enables more perp trading, which generates more on-chain fees. The result is a self-reinforcing growth loop that is fully attributable to the supporting chain.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       |

## Competition


| Question                                                            | Answer                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      |
| ------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| What alternatives exist for users for your project right now?       | The market has several adjacent categories but no direct equivalent: **Pooled-liquidity perps** (GMX, Jupiter, Gains) -- optimise for execution and pooled trading, but users interact with a venue, not a structured product. **Order-book / app-chain perps** (Hyperliquid, dYdX) -- optimise for execution depth and throughput, not structured ownership products. **Hybrid venues** (Drift, Vertex, Synthetix) -- optimise for hybrid breadth but don't combine structured exposure with shared liquidity and attribution. **Manager/strategist infrastructure** (Enzyme, Sommelier) -- support configurable vaults but lack a shared perpetual-liquidity nucleus and explicit reserve-backed redemption discipline. **Tokenized wrappers/index products** (Set Protocol, Index Coop) -- strong at ownership abstraction but not built around active liquidity design or manager extensibility.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        |
| How is your solution better than existing alternatives cited above? | IndexFlow is positioned as "Wave 5: Structured Exposure Infrastructure" -- it takes the product simplicity of tokenized wrappers, the capital efficiency of shared-liquidity systems, and the configurability of manager platforms, then reorganises them around the relationship between full NAV and redeemable liquidity. Key differentiators: - **Capital efficiency** -- high, shared execution engine across all vaults (vs. isolated fragmented liquidity). - **Redemption quality** -- reserve-backed with explicit NAV vs. redeemable liquidity separation (vs. fragile or weak redemption in alternatives). - **Manager extensibility** -- custom return schedules, custom oracles, domain-specific logic, ring-fenced reserves per vault. **Chain attribution** -- TVL, volume, and fees remain measured and ring-fenced per chain for partner attribution, while deposit routing uses hub-and-spoke coordination (keeper-posted weights + on-chain guards) and redemptions fill cross-chain via CCIP without blending chain-local KPIs. - **Liquidity durability** -- continues monitoring on-chian (vs treasury write-offs) |
