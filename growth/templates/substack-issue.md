## Tone Guide

Write as if emailing one specific investor or builder you respect. Use "you" and "we." Share one thing you learned or changed your mind about this week. Substack readers subscribe for the human behind the protocol, not the protocol itself.

- First person, conversational, honest
- Acknowledge uncertainty where it exists ("we're still figuring out..." is stronger than false confidence)
- One "behind the curtain" moment per issue -- a decision you wrestled with, a metric that surprised you, a mistake you caught
- No marketing-speak. If it could appear in a press release, rewrite it.

## Notes

- Target 500-800 words. Substack is not the blog -- keep it tighter and more personal.
- Always include at least one "why should I care" moment for a non-technical reader.
- Best send times: Tuesday or Thursday morning, 09:00-11:00 UTC.
- Use the subject line as the hook -- it's what determines open rate.
