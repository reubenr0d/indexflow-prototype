## Hook Type Reference

Pick one. The hook determines whether anyone reads the rest.

| Type | Pattern | IndexFlow Example |
| ---- | ------- | ----------------- |
| **Data** | Open with a specific number that reframes what the audience cares about | "89% of DeFi vault TVL has no explicit redemption policy. That's not a feature -- it's a bug." |
| **Contrarian** | Take a position against a widely accepted belief | "Your vault's TVL is not your exit liquidity. Most protocols pretend otherwise." |
| **Insider Knowledge** | Promise exclusive information most people don't have | "We read every on-chain basket protocol's contracts. Here's what none of them handle." |
| **Curiosity Gap** | Open incomplete so the reader needs the answer | "There's a number every vault investor should check before depositing. Almost nobody does." |
| **Stakes** | Show what readers lose by not having the information | "If your vault protocol can't tell you the difference between NAV and redeemable liquidity, you have a problem." |
| **Personal Story** | Open with a specific, genuine moment from experience | "We spent 3 months building on GMX v1. The first thing we learned is that nobody tells you..." |

**Avoid**: "Thread on how to...", "I've been thinking about...", "Here are my thoughts on...", starting with "So..." or "Okay..."

## Content Mix Guidance

At least 3 of 5 weekly tweets should be educational or insight-driven (not promotional). Follow the **60/30/10 rule** across the week:

- **60%** educational / thought leadership
- **30%** project updates and building in public
- **10%** promotional (CTA, link to testnet, join Telegram)

## Notes

- Threads get 63% more impressions than single tweets and keep readers engaged 2-3 min vs 5 sec for singles.
- No hashtag spam. Zero or one hashtag per tweet, max.
- Threads perform best posted between 14:00-17:00 UTC on weekdays.
- Quote-tweet the hook with a one-line summary 2-3 hours after posting for additional reach.
- If a tweet contains a diagram or screenshot, note it as [IMAGE: description]. Visual tweets get ~150% more engagement.
- Brand voice: "smart colleague at a conference" -- precise, systems-language, confident. Not meme-y, not corporate.
